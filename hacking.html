    • Course

    Overview

    This course is designed to provide students with a thorough understanding of ethical hacking and its role in ensuring the security of computer systems. The course covers a wide range of topics, including:

  • Introduction to Ethical Hacking
  • Penetration Testing
  • Network Security
  • Web Application Security
  • Wireless Security
  • Malware Analysis
  • Cryptography
  • Social Engineering
  • Incident Response

    • Throughout the course, you will learn the tools and techniques used by both ethical hackers and malicious attackers. By understanding the tactics used by attackers, you will be better equipped to protect systems and data from unauthorized access.

    Course

    Format

    The Ethical Hacking course is delivered through a combination of online lectures, hands-on labs, and interactive discussions. The course is self-paced, allowing you to learn at your own speed and on your own schedule.

    Each module includes a series of video lectures, followed by hands-on lab exercises to reinforce the concepts covered in the lectures. The labs provide an opportunity to practice the skills learned in the lectures in a safe and controlled environment.

    In addition to the lectures and labs, the course includes interactive discussions with the instructor and other students. These discussions provide an opportunity to ask questions, share insights, and learn from the experiences of others.

    Course

    Objectives

    By the end of this course, you will be able to:

  • Understand the fundamentals of ethical hacking and its role in ensuring the security of computer systems.
  • Perform penetration testing to identify vulnerabilities in systems and networks.
  • Implement network security measures to protect against attacks.
  • Secure web applications and prevent common web application vulnerabilities.
  • Analyze malware to understand its capabilities and develop strategies to mitigate its effects.
  • Implement cryptography to protect data and communications.
  • Use social engineering techniques to test the security of an organization's human resources.
  • Respond to security incidents and develop incident response plans.

    • Prerequisites

    This course is designed for students with a basic understanding of computer systems and networking. Familiarity with a programming language, such as Python or Java, is recommended but not required.
